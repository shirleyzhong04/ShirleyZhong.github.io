Exercise 6

Exercise 6 was an introduction to creating different scenes and implementing different libraries just as a test. I was surprised to find that implementing sound was very easy, as it is essentially the same as inputting images. SceneManager made it really easy to switch between scenes as well, as I remember from the array exercise that switching between scenes was a little different. The libraries used here are p5play.js, planck.min.js, scenemanager.js, and the sound libraries. These will be the same libraries used in my final project, so this exercise helped a lot with understanding these libraries and utilizing them.

Project 4

Welcome to the final project of the semester!! We dive into interface and audio visual language for our last project. A big part of my project specifically was theatrification. My program was made to have a calming aesthetic to it, and to do so required the entire design of the program to be, well, calming. This was done with muted colors so nothing was too vibrant. I also chose to incorporate an animal that many people enjoy having contact with - the cat. Additionally, all user interfaces are designed to be simple and non-overwhelming. Another big factor was the sounds used. There is calming music being played in the background that serves a cozy environment. Cats purring are also considered to be very calming, so why not make the program all about petting the cat and making it purr!
